DevelopersHub Internship Tasks

This repository showcases the tasks and projects I completed during my Data Science & Analytics Internship at Developers Hub Corporation.

It reflects my hands-on experience, practical implementations, and learning progress across various data science concepts, including data analysis, visualization, and machine learning.

Tasks Overview
Task 1: Exploring and Visualizing the Iris Dataset

Objective: Understand how to load, analyze, and visualize a dataset.
Approach: Utilized pandas to load the dataset and performed Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) using matplotlib and seaborn, including scatter plots, histograms, and box plots.
Results & Insights: The visualizations showed clear clustering among Iris species based on sepal and petal features. Box plots also helped identify outliers that may need treatment in future modeling.

Task 2: Credit Risk Prediction

Objective: Predict whether a loan applicant is likely to default.
Approach: Preprocessed missing values, conducted EDA on applicant data, and trained a Logistic Regression model.
Results & Insights: The model achieved an accuracy of 78.86%. Credit history emerged as the most influential factor. The confusion matrix indicated moderate risk tolerance, suggesting room for improving decision thresholds.

Task 3: Customer Churn Prediction

Objective: Identify customers at risk of leaving the bank.
Approach: Cleaned the dataset, applied One-Hot Encoding for categorical variables, and trained a Decision Tree classifier.
Results & Insights: The model reached an accuracy of 78.05%. Feature importance analysis revealed that age is the most significant factor, followed by estimated salary and account balance.

Task 4: Predicting Insurance Claim Amounts

Objective: Estimate medical insurance charges based on customer data.
Approach: Converted categorical variables into numerical format and trained a Linear Regression model.
Results & Insights: The model achieved an R² score of 0.7836 and a Mean Absolute Error of $4,181.19. Smoking status was identified as the most impactful factor, along with age and BMI.

Task 5: Personal Loan Acceptance Prediction

Objective: Predict which customers are likely to accept a personal loan offer.
Approach: Processed the Bank Marketing dataset, performed EDA, and trained a Decision Tree classifier.
Results & Insights: The duration of the last contact was the most important predictor, followed by previous campaign outcomes. Customers with longer engagement and positive history showed higher acceptance rates.
